Sun Yat-Sen
Memorial Bridge

Our newest design for our newest bridge

We are the Diocesan Architecture Pioneers. We strive to create the most stunning and practical bridges in the world. Recently, our English Teacher (Ms. Lee) has told (more like, employed) us to make a bridge which connects from DBS to MOKO (more precisely, the Mong Kok East station), a common place where DBS students eat their lunch. For this, we have come up with an unprecedented helical truss design for the bridge that will both satisfy your tummy and your feet. What's more, you will also be able to discover the fascinating stories of Dr. Sun Yat-Sen (a DBS old boy).

  • Learn More in our YouTube Video

Features

Why use our Bridge?

Well, our bridge provides faster and barrier-free access to both the MOKO shopping mall and the Mong Kok East MTR station. By doing so, it will ease congestion in the Steps and the Mini Steps. Not only that, but it also deploys renewable solar energy. It should be a good alternative to cars and helps achieve carbon neutrality.

Historical Details

One day, we passed by the Sun Yat-Sen Statue in the DBS campus. We decided to make the bridge like a History Museum, filled with fascinating stories of Dr. Sun Yat-Sen, a DBS old boy.

Architecture

We decided to use a unique architectural design, the helical truss, which is visually appealing. We used green paint to blend it in with the landscape, giving people a breathtaking view.

Other info

We used reinforced concrete and stainless steel for the helical truss, with seating areas specifically designed for parents. The distance is about 450m and there will also be free WiFi.
